Description

The Brihadeeswarar Temple, often referred to as the Big Temple, is the crowning jewel of Thanjavur and one of the most celebrated architectural marvels in India. Constructed by the Chola dynasty’s king, Raja Raja Chola I, in the 11th century, this temple is an outstanding example of Dravidian architecture. It is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is renowned for its colossal vimana, a pyramid-like structure that soars to a height of 66 meters. The temple’s architecture is awe-inspiring, with intricate carvings and sculptures that narrate tales from Hindu mythology. The colossal Nandi (bull) statue, made from a single stone, guards the entrance, and the sanctum sanctorum houses the massive Shiva Lingam. The temple’s main attraction is the Kumbam (the topmost structure of the vimana) made entirely of a single piece of granite that weighs around 80 tons.
