About Madurai District and Its History
Madurai city is the headquarters of Madurai district in Tamilnadu. This city is located on the bank of river Vaigai. Madurai is proud to have developed Tamil Language through Sangam. Madurai is well known for its cultural heritage and 3000 years old city. Madurai was the capital city of the great emperor Pandyas. Once Madurai was a forest called "Kadambavanam". Madurai was ruled by various emperors like Pandyas, Cholas, Madurai Sultanate, Vijayanagar Empire, Madurai Nayaks, Chanda Sahib, Carnatic kingdom, and British.
Meenakshi Amman Temple:
This splendid temple, known as Arulmigu Meenakshi Sundaraswarar Temple, is situated at the heart of Madurai city and was commissioned by King Kulasekhara Pandya. Notable for its stunning Raja Gopuram and the sacred Potramarai Kulam, this temple is a true highlight. It's conveniently located near the Madurai city bus stand.
Thirumalai Nayakkar Palace:
Located a short distance from the Madurai Meenakshi temple, the Thirumalai Nayakkar Mahal boasts exquisite architecture dating back around 370 years. Visitors are welcome between 9 am and 5 pm, and they can explore the history of Thirumalai Nayakkar through an audio-visual presentation.
Gandhi Museum:
The Rani Mangammal Palace was renovated to create a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. The same premises also house the Government Museum, established in 1981, which safeguards various art objects, sculptures, and documents. Visitors can explore these treasures from 10 am to 6 pm, with Fridays as a designated holiday.
Rajaji Park:
Situated near the Madurai Corporation Office, Rajaji Park is a beautifully designed space suitable for visitors of all ages. The park offers a range of features for both children and adults to enjoy and is accessible from 10 am to 8 pm.
