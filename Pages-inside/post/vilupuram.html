About Viluppuram District and Its History
Viluppuram is the administrative headquarters of the Viluppuram district. Ramasamy Padayachi District was declared and later renamed as viluppuram District. The geographical extend of Villupuram district is surrounded on East and south by Cuddalore district, west by kallakurichi district, north by Thiruvannamalai and Kanchipuram district. Agriculture is the main source of income in this district. 75% of people engaged in agriculture. Paddy, Blackgram, and Sugarcane are the major crops cultivated in this district. Govt. medical college, Mailam Engineering College and Claribel Matriculation School are the important educational institution in Villupuram District.
Gingee Fort
Gingee Fort, also known as the Red Fort, is a historic fortification situated on a vast area amidst three hills: Rajagiri, Chandragiri, and Krishnagiri. It was constructed in the late 12th century and has been associated with several notable rulers, with Desingurajan being one of them.
Tirukoilur / Tirukovilur
Tirukoilur, or Tirukovilur, is a renowned Vaishnava temple in the region. The main deities worshipped here are Sri Ulagalanda Perumal and Pushpavalli Amman. It is located approximately 38 km from Villupuram and is administered by the Government of Tamil Nadu. Buses from Thiruvannamalai and Chittoor provide convenient access to Tirukovilur. Nearby, there is Kabilar Kundru, which is believed to be the residence of the poet Kabilar.
Singavaram
Singavaram features a cave temple atop a hill in Senji, where Desingurajan is said to have worshiped. The primary deity in this temple is Aranganathar Perumal, and the main idol is carved from a single stone.
Shri Ulagalantha Perumal Temple
The Shri Ulagalantha Perumal Temple boasts a tall gopuram that adds to its majestic appearance, reminiscent of the grandeur of the Great Temple of Tanjore.
