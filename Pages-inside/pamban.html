Description

The Pamban Bridge is a notable railway bridge located in the southern Indian state of Tamil Nadu. It connects the town of Rameswaram on Pamban Island to the mainland at Mandapam. This bridge is a vital transportation link that plays a crucial role in connecting Rameswaram Island to the Indian mainland. The Pamban Bridge is renowned for its unique design. It is a cantilever bridge that was originally constructed in 1914 and later reconstructed to its current form in 1964. What makes it particularly fascinating is that it features a "double-leaf bascule" section, which can be lifted to allow ships and ferries to pass through. This is necessary because the bridge stands over the Palk Strait, a body of water that sees regular maritime traffic. The bridge not only serves as a functional transportation route but also offers spectacular views of the surrounding area, including the mesmerizing waters of the Palk Strait.
