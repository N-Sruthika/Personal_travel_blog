Description

Guna Cave, also known as the "Devil's Kitchen," is a famous tourist site located in Kodaikanal, a picturesque hill station in Tamil Nadu, India. It comprises a series of deep caves and chambers nestled between three massive boulders. The cave earned its ominous nickname, "Devil's Kitchen," due to a tragic incident in 1903 when a group of people, including British officer B.S. Ward, became lost inside the cave. This incident led to the official closure of the cave to ensure public safety. Surrounded by lush forests, Guna Cave provides an intriguing environment for exploration. However, because of safety concerns, it is generally off-limits to tourists. Nevertheless, visitors can still appreciate the natural beauty of the area from the outside and take in the serene and green landscapes of Kodaikanal, which is renowned for its cool climate and scenic vistas.
