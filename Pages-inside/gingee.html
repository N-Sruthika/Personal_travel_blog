Description

Gingee Fort, also known as Senji Fort, is a historic and imposing fort complex located in the Viluppuram district of Tamil Nadu, India. This remarkable fortress stands as a testament to the architectural prowess and military significance of the past, and it has a rich history that spans several centuries.The fort is known for its strategic location and its remarkable architecture, which blends a variety of styles, including Dravidian and Rajput.Gingee Fort has a storied history, with several notable events taking place within its walls. It was a stronghold for various regional rulers, including Krishnadevaraya of the Vijayanagara Empire and the Marathas. The fort also played a key role in the Carnatic Wars between the British and the French in the 18th century.The complex is divided into three main hills, each featuring its own fortification and unique elements:Rajagiri: This hill is the highest and most prominent part of the fort complex;Krishnagiri: The Krishnagiri hill is home to the Ranganatha Temple;Chakkilidrug: Chakkilidrug is the smallest of the three hills.
